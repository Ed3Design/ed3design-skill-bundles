# Code of Conduct

This project is small enough that we don't need a formal pledge — but we do want to be clear about expectations.

## What this is

A repository of empirically-tested engineering discipline patterns, distributed as Claude Code skill bundles.

## What we want from contributors

- **Be specific.** Don't say "this is wrong" — say "this is wrong because X, and here's a failing test that demonstrates it."
- **Be concrete with evidence.** "I'd prefer Y over X" → "Here's a benchmark / repro / spec link showing Y is the right call."
- **Disagree without escalation.** Technical disagreement is welcome and expected. Personal attacks are not.
- **Assume good faith.** If a contribution looks wrong, ask "what was your reasoning?" before concluding the author was negligent.
- **Time-box thread escalation.** If two contributors are stuck after three exchanges, ping a maintainer to break the tie.

## What gets things removed

- Personal attacks, harassment, doxxing.
- Spam, off-topic political content, or repeated promotion of unrelated projects.
- Deliberate disclosure of sensitive information (credentials, private keys, internal company data — even your own).
- Knowingly submitting backdoored or exploitable code disguised as a contribution.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may close issues, delete comments, or block accounts that violate the above. We will explain why in writing (publicly or privately) when we do so.

## Reporting

Conflict that needs maintainer attention: open an issue with the `meta` label, or contact maintainers privately via the email listed in `SECURITY.md` (we reuse that channel for both topics — it's a small project).
